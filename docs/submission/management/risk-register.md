# Risk Register

Likelihood scale: Low, Medium, High. Impact scale: Medium, High, Critical.

| ID | Risk Description | Likelihood | Impact | What Breaks if It Happens | Mitigation Strategy | Contingency Plan | Primary Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| R-01 | Groq is unavailable, rate-limited, or too slow during testing or demo windows. | Medium | High | Rewrite, summarize, translate, and restructure flows become unavailable; the product loses a core differentiator during evaluation. | Keep backend-level timeouts, retries, and clear `503` and `504` error codes; validate limits early; keep a mock AI mode for local and demo use. | Switch to mock responses for the demo, disable live AI in the UI with a clear banner, and use recorded evidence of the end-to-end path if the outage is upstream. | Temiko |
| R-02 | AI usage cost exceeds the team's planned budget because prompts are too large or use grows faster than expected. | Medium | Medium | AI features may need to be throttled or disabled unexpectedly, which affects scope commitments and user trust. | Enforce per-request token caps, daily per-user quotas, and organization-level budget limits; keep default prompts selection-scoped instead of full-document. | Reduce context windows, downgrade non-critical features to a cheaper model, and temporarily reserve AI for owner/editor roles only. | Temiko |
| R-03 | CRDT synchronization produces inconsistent or surprising merged state under concurrent edits. | Low | Critical | The editor's core trust model fails; users can lose confidence in document correctness or see corrupted text. | Write deterministic two-client and multi-client conflict tests, persist snapshots regularly, and keep merge logic inside proven Yjs primitives rather than custom reconciliation code. | Freeze new collaboration work, restore from the latest valid snapshot if needed, and isolate the failing edit pattern with a regression test before re-enabling rollout. | Tanisha and Teya |
| R-04 | The single-instance `y-websocket` server becomes a bottleneck or single point of failure. | Low | High | Real-time edits and presence fail even if the REST API and database remain healthy; collaboration degrades to offline/local-only behavior. | Document the scale ceiling, add health checks and restart policies, and keep persistence decoupled enough to support a future Hocuspocus plus Redis upgrade path. | Continue local editing with reconnect sync when the service recovers, and formally scope horizontal scaling out of the semester MVP if the bottleneck appears earlier than expected. | Teya |
| R-05 | Frontend and backend drift away from the agreed API and schema contract. | Medium | High | The proof of concept may compile but fail end-to-end, especially around document payloads, auth refresh, permissions, and AI streaming states. | Treat Pydantic schemas as the contract source, generate shared types where possible, and gate merges on integration tests for happy-path plus error-path behavior. | Declare a short contract-alignment freeze, reconcile breaking payload changes first, then resume feature work only after contract tests pass in CI. | Atharv and Tanisha |
| R-06 | Monorepo coordination creates merge conflicts or hidden ownership gaps on cross-cutting features. | Medium | Medium | Progress slows, reviews get blocked, and changes to shared files can destabilize unrelated areas. | Keep directory ownership explicit, prefer small feature branches, require cross-owner review on shared contracts, and write short ADRs for decisions that span multiple areas. | Pair on conflict resolution, re-slice large PRs into smaller merges, and temporarily freeze churn in shared directories while the critical branch lands. | Entire team |
| R-07 | A team member becomes unavailable during a key sprint and their subsystem lacks handoff documentation. | Medium | Medium | Delivery risk concentrates on one area such as AI, infrastructure, or API integration; the remaining team loses time rediscovering assumptions. | Maintain setup instructions, ADRs, and repository ownership notes; pair during Sprint 1 and Sprint 2 so at least one secondary owner exists for each major area. | Reassign the milestone around the documented interfaces, defer lower-value scope, and convert missing knowledge into written handoff notes before the next sprint starts. | Entire team |

## Risk Review Cadence

- Revisit the register during sprint planning and sprint review.
- Escalate any risk that moves to High likelihood or Critical impact into a tracked GitHub issue with an owner and due date.
- Treat unresolved API drift, CRDT correctness bugs, and AI reliability regressions as release blockers rather than cosmetic issues.
